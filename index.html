We are building permanence for a renewable grid.

The energy transition doesn’t fail for lack of electrons. It fails when those electrons are late, lost, or locked. Intermittency is not a nuisance—it is the constraint. The grid needs storage that behaves like infrastructure, not consumables.

The problem

Today’s grid-scale storage leans on short-lived chemistries, complex supply chains, fire risk, and degradation curves that compound into operational drag. We curtail renewables, overbuild networks, and still pay for scarcity.

This is misallocation at continental scale.

The premise

A durable grid demands a durable store: abundant materials, simple physics, long life, and stable performance across decades. Storage must be civil, not disposable—measured in years and cycles, not firmware versions.

The answer

Bubble is high-temperature compressed-air energy storage. We store energy as pressurised air and high-grade heat—no lithium, no combustion, no rare metals. Built like infrastructure; operated like a power asset.

What this enables

Firm power from variable renewables. Fewer curtailment hours. Higher capture rates. Lower system cost.

In practice: long-duration flexibility using planetary materials—air, rock, steel, ceramics.

Principles we refuse to compromise

1) Longevity over replacement. Design life in decades, not product cycles.

2) Safety by physics. Non-flammable working media. Fail-safe architectures.

3) Material honesty. Abundant inputs. No hostage supply chains.

4) Thermodynamic respect. Minimise entropy creation; capture useful heat.

5) Grid fit. Modularity, siting flexibility, and civil-works pragmatism.

Commitment

We will validate openly with independent partners. We will publish what matters. We will prove performance at meaningful scale and invite scrutiny.

Where we are

Advancing toward an integrated TRL-6 demonstration with TNO in the Netherlands. Industrial partners are aligned; the pilot pathway is defined.

What we ask

If you build, finance, regulate, or operate the future grid—and you believe storage should be infrastructure—work with us.
